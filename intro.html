Definition

Technology is the use of scientific knowledge to solve human problems and make life more comfortable. The word technology comes from two Greek words: Tekhnē – meaning art or craft. Logia – meaning study or science. So, technology literally means “the science or study of crafting.”

Technology includes both the processes (methods) and products (materials) that make life easier and less stressful. It is the practical use of scientific knowledge to create tools and machines that help people meet their needs

  • When we apply science to solve other people’s problems, it is called technology.
  • When we apply science to solve our personal problems, it remains science, not technology.

Types of Technology

  • Developed Technology

    • Uses modern scientific methods to solve problems. Examples: airplanes, cars, trains for transportation.

  • Under-Developed Technology

    • Relies on traditional or indigenous methods to meet needs. Examples: using a horse-drawn cart or trekking for transportation

  • Developing Technology

    • Combines some aspects of developed technology with traditional methods. Example: countries like Nigeria that use both modern and indigenous technology.

Technology-Related Professions

  • Electrical and Electronics Profession

    • Involves assembling, installing, testing, troubleshooting, and repairing electrical wiring, fixtures, control devices, and equipment in buildings or structures.

  • Mechanical Technician Profession

    • Includes refrigeration and air conditioning repairs, elevator installation, servicing, and repair of mechanical equipment.

  • Building Professions

    • This covers many areas such as: Plumber: Installs and repairs pipes, fixtures, and plumbing equipment for water distribution. ,Bricklayer: Builds or repairs walls, chimneys, and ovens using blocks, bricks, stone, or concrete. ,Painter and Decorator: Applies paint, wallpaper, and other finishes to building surfaces. ,Roofer: Installs, repairs, or replaces roofs. ,Blueprint Interpreter: Reads and interprets building plans. ,Tile Setter: Covers floors, walls, and ceilings with ceramics, marble, or terrazzo. ,Glazier: Cuts, fits, and installs glass in buildings.

Importance of Technology

  • Improves the standard of living.
  • Increases productivity and efficiency.
  • Makes communication easier and faster.
  • Promotes safety in life and living.
  • Helps protect the environment.
  • Saves time, energy, and resources.
  • Makes movement and transportation easy.
  • Supports entertainment, business, and industry.
  • Helps preserve important information and data.
  • Improves learning by providing access to knowledge worldwide.

Importance of Technological Literacy

  • Encourages creativity in students.
  • Provides a strong foundation for future vocational or technical training.
  • Gives students basic knowledge of technology.
  • Promotes technological development from the grassroots level.
  • Helps students become self-reliant.
